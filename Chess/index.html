Chess AI

This chess project is very close to my heart. A while back, I got into a month or two-long kick of playing chess every day and researching how chess engines actually work. I was fascinated by the complexity behind even the simplest moves and wanted to understand what made chess bots so good. That curiosity eventually turned into a challenge I set for myself: build a chess engine from scratch, entirely in JavaScript, running in the browser.

It wasn’t easy. Writing a game like this in a single-threaded environment brings a lot of performance limitations, and making it efficient took a lot of experimenting and problem-solving. But after a lot of time and iteration, I ended up with something I’m genuinely proud of a chess bot that can at least beat me. Still, it plays decently, and I hope you have fun trying to beat it. This project taught me a lot and reminded me why I love programming. I poured a lot into it, and I absolutely love how it turned out.
