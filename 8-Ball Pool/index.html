8-Ball Pool With AI

This was a project I created at the end of my freshman year of college. I built both the UI and the AI completely from scratch using vanilla JavaScript, without relying on any libraries! While this approach wasn’t the most practical, it worked, and I’m happy with the result. It’s worth noting that several rules from real-life billiards are missing here—this is an 8-Ball game, not an exact simulation. I made it purely to learn and have fun.

The AI works by simulating thousands of potential future shots it could take. For each of these hypothetical shots, it evaluates the outcome and assigns a score based on how favorable it is. The AI then chooses the shot with the highest score.

That said, the AI is far from perfect. It uses a brute-force algorithm, which isn’t the most efficient approach. For a problem like this, an extensive amount of data about the cue ball's state is needed to make consistently good decisions. While the AI occasionally makes incredible shots, it also struggles with some "easy" ones. Despite its flaws, this is one of my favorite projects to date. Also, while I’m calling this an AI, it’s really a catch-all term for the algorithms I’ve designed for the game.
